About the Crop:

Banana (Musa species) Bhusan (Robusta) plants typically yield fruit 9 to 12 months after planting. They thrive in well-drained, fertile loamy soil with a pH of 5.5 to 7.0. Each acre can produce about 30 to 40 tons of bananas annually. Water needs are around 1,200 to 1,500 mm per year, with consistent moisture crucial for fruit development. Costs for cultivation, including planting material, fertilizers, irrigation, and labor, range from ₹1,00,000 to ₹1,50,000 per acre. With market prices averaging ₹20 to ₹30 per kg, net profit can range from ₹4,00,000 to ₹8,00,000 per acre, depending on yield and market conditions. Proper management practices ensure healthy plants and optimal returns.

  • Soil Composition: Sandy-loam and Clayey-loam Soil with Ph 5.5 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 1200-1500 mm per acre
  • Potential Dangers: Nematodes, Mycosphaerella fijiensis, BBTV
  • Fertilizers: Di-Ammonium Phosphate, Magnesium Sulfate, Foliar Feeding:
  • Pesticides: Imidacloprid, Lambda-Cyhalothrin, Furadan
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹4,35,000 to ₹11,00,000 per acre annually
